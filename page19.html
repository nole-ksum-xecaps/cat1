Krautheimer was a refugee from the Nazis who came to the United States in the 1930s. Much of his career was spent as a professor in the Institute of Fine Arts at NYU. After he retired, he moved to Rome. The book from which we are reading was the product of his lifelong studies of Roman churches and of his love for the city. Into his 90s, he walked every day from his apartment near the Piazza di Spagna in the northern part of the old city to the Vatican Library to do research for this book and some scholarly projects. As noted, while you read this work you will find Krautheimer using terms that part of the language of art history. When you come across a word that puzzles you or that you think you need to know to understand the point he is making, look it up. I do the same when I read Krautheimer. The effort is worth it. This book will probably be the first great scholarly work you read. Among other things, it is your introduction to what serious scholars do.19
