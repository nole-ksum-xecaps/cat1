Introduction to Ancient RomeStanley ChodorowThe place where Rome grew into a great city formed at the end of the last ice age, about 11,700 years ago. As the glaciers in the Apennine mountains melted, a “river” between .625 to 1.25 miles wide and about 100 feet deep flowed down to the sea roughly along the course of the modern Tiber River. This tremendous flow carved hills out from the table land near the coast leaving seven hills later incorporated into the city of Rome.
