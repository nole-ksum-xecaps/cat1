Martino ai Monti, Rome the apse vault of Constantine's church shim-mered in gold; gold and silver lighting fixtures and seven silver tables-one Qresumably the al-tar, the other to hold offerings-filled nave and chancel; on the chord of the apse, a colonnaded, gabled screen, or a canopy, sheathed in silver, sheltered or carried statues of Christ, the Apos-tles, and angels. Behind the church, built over part of an older mansion, the baptistery still rises, dating presumably about 315; its inside was re-modeled a century later.
