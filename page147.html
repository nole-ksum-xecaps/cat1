As he cleared buildings for his big roads and for his parks, he straightened and widened many other roads. Today, you have to go to the Left Bank around the old University of Paris neighborhoods to find the crooked streets that were characteristic of the whole city when Haussmann arrived in town.Also in chapter 2, you’ll see a reference to the annexation of the suburbs in 1860, with a map that shows the areas incorporated into city. The annexation led to a reorganization of the city’s districts—the arrondissements. Paris had had arrondissements for administrative purposes for hundreds of years, but Haussmann reorganized them after he annexed the suburbs. These new areas were developed to house the people displaced by the clearing of densely populated parts of the central city. In Haussmann’s time, the population of Paris increased substantially, and he accommodated the new people with new territory and urbandevelopment while preserving his vision of the city’s center.
