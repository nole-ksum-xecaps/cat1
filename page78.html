In the 8th century, theyrenovated and rebuilt some important churches. In that period, the popes had the active support of the new emperors, beginning with Charlemagne. During the reform of the 11thcentury, the popes rebuilt a number of churches to attract pilgrims, who brought money as well as prestige and attention, and to make claims to control these churches. Local families, which dominated their neighborhoods, had controlled the churches, collecting whatever revenue they produced and appointing the priests who presided over them. The renovations were assertions of the bishop’s authority over the churches, consistent with the reform program. Although the records do not prove the case, it appears that the assertion of authority resulted in papalcontrol over the renovated, and eventually all, churches in the diocese. By the middle of the 12th century, the popes controlled all the churches in Rome, and the powerful local families played their politics by becoming involved in the papacy. Virtually all of them had members among the Roman clergy.The periodic building booms in the medieval city diminished the ancient monuments in the forum area. The medieval builders pillaged the ancient buildings for stones. Why make new building materials when so much was lying around? In the 15th and 16th centuries, when there was large-scale building in the city, the masons especially took the marble facades from the ancient structures, some of which had been clad in marble by Augustus. Look at the Colosseum today, and you will see a few slabs of marble on its façade. The remainder of what was once on the building is scattered around the city. The population of the city grew as the papal government grew. After the 1122 agreement, the church was an independent, international institution with its government, centered in Constantine’s church of St. Peter on Vatican hill. By the mid-12th century it had grown into an international power. By the 13th century, the papacy was the largest and most sophisticated government in Europe, exercising power throughout the church and drawing people and wealth to Rome. The city, in its post-6th-century area north of the ancient forum, became a dense urban establishment. This was the Rome of the 16th-century popes, who reorganized the city to accommodate the crush of pilgrims the popes actively encouraged to come, as well as the crowd of people who came to do business with the papal government.78
