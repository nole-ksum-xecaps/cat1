The 1st and 2nd centuries C.E. were periods in which Rome ruled a vast empire that circled the Mediterranean, which the Romans called Mare Nostrum (our sea), and reached into northern Europe—France, Belgium, England, the edge of western Germany—and eastern Europe—Austria, Romania, Bulgaria. But by the end of the 2nd century there were new threats the emperors had to deal with. Germanic tribes had been migrating from central Asia into the forests of northern Europe and began to make incursions into the western European provinces of the empire. The comparative wealth of the provinces attracted their attention. By the mid-3rd century, a resurgence of Persia threatened the eastern provinces, which were the most populous and richest in the empire.The military stresses greatly enlarged the army and forced Rome to build an expensive fortified frontier across northern and eastern Europe and in the Middle East.
